INTRODUCTION OF GOOGLE

Google, an American multinational technology company, is primarily known for its search engine, which handles over 70% of online searches globally. Beyond search, Google develops and provides a wide range of products and services, including online advertising, cloud computing, software, and hardware. It was founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin as a research project called "BackRub" at Stanford University.

CORE BUSINESS :

SEARCH ENGINE

Google Search is the most widely used web search engine, providing access to a vast amount of information online.

CLOUD COMPUTING

Google Cloud offers a suite of services for businesses and individuals, including storage, computing, and data analytics.

KEY ASPECTS:

GLOBAL RESEARCH

Google's products and services are used worldwide, with offices and operations in numerous countries

HISTORY

The company evolved from a research project called BackRub, created by Larry Page and Sergey Brin at Stanford University.

INOVATION :

Google is known for its focus on innovation and developing new technologies, including in areas like quantum computing.

MISSION

Google's mission is "to organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful."

STRUCTURE

In 2015, Google restructured to become the main subsidiary of a holding company called Alphabet Inc. Sundar Pichai is the current CEO of both Google and Alphabet Inc.
