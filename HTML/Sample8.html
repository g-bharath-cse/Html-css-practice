Padma Vibhushan Mr. Ratan N Tata 28.12.1937 – 09.10.2024

Ratan Naval Tata[a] (28 December 1937 – 9 October 2024) was an Indian industrialist and philanthropist. He served as the chairman of Tata Group and Tata Sons from 1991 to 2012 and he held the position of interim chairman from October 2016 to February 2017.[3][4] In 2000, he received the Padma Bhushan, the third highest civilian honour in India, followed by the Padma Vibhushan, the country's second highest civilian honour, in 2008.[5]

ABOUT TATA MOTORS

Tata Motors is a leading global automobile manufacturer and part of the Tata Group, a large Indian conglomerate. They produce a wide range of vehicles, including passenger cars, utility vehicles, trucks, buses, and also have a presence in electric vehicles and defense vehicles. Tata Motors is a major player in the Indian market, particularly in commercial vehicles, and is also among the top three in passenger vehicles.

What is the history of Tata company?

The Tata Group, founded by Jamsetji Tata in 1868, began as a trading company and has grown into one of India's largest conglomerates. Jamsetji's vision included establishing an iron and steel company, a unique hotel, a world-class learning institution, and a hydroelectric plant. Over the years, Tata expanded into various sectors like steel, automobiles, IT services, hospitality, and more.

What is the logo of Tata?

The Tata logo is a blue oval with a stylized, uppercase "T" in the center. The "T" is often depicted in a bold, clean font, and is sometimes enclosed within a circle or other symbolic shape. The logo represents the Tata Group, a global conglomerate, and is associated with values like excellence, reliability, and strength.
